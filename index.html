  • ⠿
    1
    Terminology
    ×
    (There are several parties, courses and manual steps involved.  Need consistent words for this stuff.)

    Donor: Instructor who built the original course and modifies it prior to source course handoff.

    Recipient: Instructor who receives the source course and personalizes it as their own.

    Original course: Donor's own course that serves as the basis for the source course to be modified for handoff.

    Source Development course: The donor-modified copy of their own original course.

    Source Template: A "master" (sorry) copy of each source course for reuse (managed by TMI)

    Recipient Development course(?): The recipient-modified copy of the source template course. 

    Donor Handoff: Process of TMI obtaining the source course from the donor (donor pre-handoff cleanup > TMI review > TMI post-handoff cleanup > TMI copy to template).

    Recipient Handoff: Process of TMI providing source course to recipient (adoption shell created > recipient enrolled > source imported >TMI consult).

    Pre-flight: Required manual edits by the Recipient to personalize the course.

    Develop step-by-step of all parts of the process. (see cards below)
    ×
    There are three parties involved, with responsibilities along the process.  Map out order and who does what:

    Donor:
    - Completes Pre-Handoff Checklist in source course.
    - Informs TMI when source course is ready for handoff

    TMI:
    - Communicates with donor re: source dev course, Pre-Handoff review.
    - Creates course shells for process (TBD who does what/when)
    - Handles course handoffs from donor > TMI/TMI > recipient

    Recipient:
    - modifies adoption dev course (RSI, etc.)
    - copies adoption dev course into official course


    Several steps involve course copies (and corresponding shells to create):
    - Original course > Source Dev course, for Pre-Handoff edits by donor. (Donor)
    - Source Dev course > Source Template,  managed by TMI, reusable. (TMI)
    - Source Template > Recipient Dev course, for Recipient (TMI)
    - Recipient Dev course > Official course, for publish and delivery to students (Recipient)

    ✕

    - If not complete, TMI notifies donor or any remaining references, dates, or course-specific content the donor missed.  TMI works with donor to remedy, as needed.
